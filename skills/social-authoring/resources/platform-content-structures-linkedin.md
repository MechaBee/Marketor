# Platform Content Structures

Platform-specific content templates and formatting guidance for social media posts.

## Metadata Format

All content files use a markdown header (NOT YAML frontmatter) for metadata:

```markdown
# [Post Title/Hook]

## Post Metadata

**Platform**: linkedin
**Format**: li-post
**Topic**: travel-insurance-benefits
**Context**: erste-cc
**Revision**: 1
**Target Audience**: young-travelers
**Objective**: engagement
**CTA URL**: https://example.com/products/
**Hashtags**: #TravelInsurance, #YourBrand, #SafeTravel
**Created**: 2025-10-28
**Status**: draft

---

## Post Content

[Platform-specific content structure follows...]
```

## LinkedIn Platform

### LinkedIn Post

```markdown
## Post Content

## Hook (First Line)
[Scroll-stopping first line - controversial, question, or bold statement]

## Context (Body Paragraphs)
[Problem setup or story context - 2-3 short paragraphs]

[Use line breaks for readability]

[Professional yet conversational tone]

## The "Aha" Moment / The Lesson
[Key takeaway or insight clearly stated]

[Actionable advice or framework]

## Engagement Prompt (Final Paragraph)
[Direct question to encourage comments]

[Invite audience perspective or experience]

---

**Hashtags:** #Hashtag1 #Hashtag2 #Hashtag3 (3-5 max)
```

**Length Optimization:**
- **Optimal**: 150-300 words (1,200-1,500 characters)
- **Maximum engagement**: Posts under 1,300 characters
- **Critical**: First 100-150 characters visible before "see more" (mobile: 100-120 chars)
- **Sweet spot**: 500-1,300 characters balances depth with readability
- **Short-form**: 150-500 characters (quick insights, questions)
- **Medium-form**: 500-1,300 characters (stories, frameworks) ‚Üê Best engagement
- **Long-form**: 1,300-3,000 characters (thought leadership, detailed analysis)

**Platform Tips:**
- First line = click-through driver (optimize first 100-150 characters)
- Story-driven content performs best
- Professional but human tone
- Line breaks crucial (desktop + mobile)
- Hashtags: 3-5 relevant, niche tags
- Engagement prompt boosts reach
- Hook must work standalone (appears in feed preview)

### LinkedIn Article

```markdown
## Post Content

## Subtitle
[Compelling subtitle expanding on title - preview text]

## Introduction
[Hook with story, statistic, or provocative question]

[Problem or topic setup in 2-3 paragraphs]

## Section 1: [Section Title]
[Main point with supporting details]

[Use subheadings, bullets, short paragraphs]

## Section 2: [Section Title]
[Second main point with examples or data]

[Include case studies or practical applications]

## Section 3: [Section Title]
[Third main point with actionable insights]

[Provide framework or step-by-step guidance]

## Conclusion
[Summarize key takeaways - 2-3 bullets]

[Thought-provoking question or call-to-action]

---

**About the Author:** [Brief bio establishing credibility]

**Hashtags:** #Hashtag1 #Hashtag2 #Hashtag3
```

**Platform Tips:**
- Long-form thought leadership (800-2000 words)
- Professional depth + accessibility
- Subheadings every 3-4 paragraphs
- Bullets and formatting for scannability
- Original insights or frameworks
- Include visuals (images, charts)
- Newsletter promotion opportunity
- SEO-friendly (searchable on Google)
